# Money reconciliation — legacy invoices vs the new tax engine

_Generated by `tools/DbReconcile` (PHASE-5-PLAN slice 6). Re-run it to refresh._

## Method

A sample of existing legacy invoices (`data_origin <> 'new'`, most recent first) is
recomputed through the **same** `Smartnet.Domain.TaxEngine` the new app issues documents
with, and the result is diffed against the total the legacy app stored in
`invoice_h.totamount`. Each invoice is fed its own stored VAT rate (`vper`) and
document discount (`discountper`) via a rate override, so the comparison isolates the one
thing under test — **binary `double` (legacy) vs `decimal` (new) arithmetic** — rather than
whether the current rate table still covers each invoice's date.

This is read-only; it writes nothing to the database and dumps no customer data.

## Proposed policy (for sign-off)

Per [LEGACY-DATA-POLICY.md](../LEGACY-DATA-POLICY.md) (decision 7), legacy figures are
**left as-is** and imported as opening balances — this reconciliation does not rewrite
them. The proposed reading of the numbers above:

- **Sub-penny differences are expected and accepted.** They are the residue of the legacy
  app's `double` math; the new `decimal` figure is the correct one, and new documents
  carry it. No remediation.
- **Any difference above a materiality threshold** (proposed: > 1.00 in document currency)
  is a legacy data defect, not a rounding artefact — it surfaces in the **Data Exceptions**
  screen for the business to correct when it chooses, exactly as the duplicate payments and
  negative balances do. It is **not** fixed silently by this migration.

_Sign-off: pending business review of the figures above._
